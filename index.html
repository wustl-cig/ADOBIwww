ADOBI: Adaptive Diffusion Bridge For Blind Inverse Problems
with Application to MRI Reconstruction

ADOBI is the first diffusion bridge method for blind imaging inverse problems.

Yuyang Hu1, Albert Peng1, Weijie Gan1, Ulugbek S. Kamilov1

1WashU

Yuyang Hu and Albert Peng contributed equally to this project.

Preprint

Abstract

Diffusion bridges (DB) have emerged as a promising alternative to diffusion models for imaging inverse problems, achieving faster sampling by directly bridging low- and high-quality image distributions. While incorporating measurement consistency has been shown to improve performance, existing DB methods fail to maintain this consistency in blind inverse problems, where the forward model is unknown. To address this limitation, we introduce ADOBI (Adaptive Diffusion Bridge for Inverse Problems), a novel framework that adaptively calibrates the unknown forward model to enforce measurement consistency throughout sampling iterations. Our adaptation strategy allows ADOBI to achieve high-quality parallel magnetic resonance imaging (PMRI) reconstruction in only 5â€“10 steps. Our numerical results show that ADOBI consistently delivers state-of-the-art performance, and further advances the Pareto frontier for the perception-distortion trade-off.
